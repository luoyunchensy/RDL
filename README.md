# Relation Discrimination Learning for Zero-Shot Relation Triplet Extraction

This repository contains the implementation of RDL (Relation Discrimination Learning), a framework for zero-shot relation triplet extraction.

## Abstract
Zero-shot relation triplet extraction (ZeroRTE) aims to identify and extract structured triplets from natural language sentences without prior exposure to target relations during training. While large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated strong performance across various zero-shot tasks, their application to relation extraction remains challenging due to limited structured information extraction capabilities. Existing approaches either rely on complex multi-stage pipelines or require large-scale models with high computational costs, often underperforming compared to supervised methods. We propose Relation Discrimination Learning (RDL), a simple yet effective framework that transforms relation classification into relation discrimination through candidate relation construction. Instead of learning to classify relations directly, our approach learns to discriminate among candidate relations, compelling models to select correct relations from candidate sets rather than performing simple classification. Crucially, we demonstrate that discrimination ability—not just relation identification—is the key constraint limiting LLM performance on this task. Extensive experiments show that RDL achieves significant improvements over state-of-the-art baselines across different model scales and datasets, maintaining excellent performance even in low-resource scenarios while requiring no complex architectural modifications. Our work validates that enhancing relation discrimination capability constitutes the fundamental breakthrough needed for successful zero-shot relation triplet extraction.
